National Security Overview
The Federal Investigation and Security Agency (FISA) operates as a centralized federal intelligence and enforcement body responsible for detecting, disrupting, and neutralizing threats to national security.

FISA integrates cyber intelligence, counter-terrorism response, field operations, and internal oversight into a unified command structure designed for rapid deployment and inter-agency coordination.

All personnel operate under strict operational security (OPSEC). Unauthorized access, disclosure, or misuse of classified information is subject to immediate review and disciplinary protocol.
